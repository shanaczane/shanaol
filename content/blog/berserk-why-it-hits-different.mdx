I've been reading Berserk since I was sixteen.

I'm not going to pretend I understood it back then. I read it because someone online said it was the darkest manga they'd ever read and I was exactly the kind of person who needed to prove I could handle it. I mostly could not handle it. I kept going anyway.

## what it's actually about

The short version: a swordsman named Guts wanders a crumbling medieval world hunting the man who betrayed him. Everything he loves gets destroyed. He keeps moving.

The long version is 374 chapters and counting.

What Miura-sensei built over thirty years wasn't just a dark fantasy story. It was a meditation on *will* — on what it means to keep living when the world has given you every reason not to. Guts doesn't survive because he's special. He survives because he *refuses not to*.

> "Even if we painstakingly piece together something lost, it doesn't mean things will ever go back to how they were."

That line hits differently every time I reread it. It hits differently at different ages. Right now, Chapter 374 era, it's hitting at a frequency I don't have words for.

## on studio gaga continuing the work

When Miura passed in 2021, I didn't think I'd ever be okay with anyone else touching Berserk. His assistants at Studio Gaga have been continuing it under the supervision of Kouji Mori, his closest friend.

Here's my honest take after three years of reading their chapters:

**They're doing right by him.**

It's not identical. It can't be. Miura's line work had this weight to it — every panel felt like it had been worried over for weeks, because it had been. Studio Gaga's work is cleaner in some places, rougher in others. But the *soul* is there.

The Fantasia arc is building to something. Chapter 374 made that clearer than any chapter since the continuation began.

### what chapter 374 specifically does

I'm going to stay vague because I don't want to spoil it, but:

- It recontextualizes something from early in the manga in a way that feels inevitable in retrospect
- A character who has been background presence for fifty chapters steps forward
- The final page made me close my laptop and sit in the dark for ten minutes

That last one is a Berserk tradition at this point. I should be used to it. I'm not.

## the art is an argument

One thing I don't see talked about enough: Berserk is one of the few manga where the *art itself* is making an argument about the story.

The way Miura drew Guts's body over the course of the series — increasingly damaged, increasingly held together by sheer spite — tells you everything about the theme before you read a single word. He looks like someone who *should have stopped* but didn't.

The Beast of Darkness sequences use a completely different visual language. Crosshatching so dense it reads as static. Panel borders that break and bleed. It's not just atmosphere — it's Guts's interior state rendered as graphic design.

Studio Gaga has maintained this. The recent chapters still know when to let the art breathe and when to make it suffocate.

## why I keep coming back

Honestly? I keep reading because Berserk makes me feel like my own stubborn refusal to give up on things is legible. Like it has a shape.

Guts doesn't have a philosophy. He doesn't have speeches about hope or destiny. He just keeps swinging. And somehow that's more sustaining than any amount of optimism.

Chapter 374 reminded me why this story matters to me. Still processing. Probably will be for a while.
